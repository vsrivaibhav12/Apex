The Rieter P 3-1 is easily the largest populated spinning top arm in the world today. This is a pneumatically loaded top arm with the benefit that even upon regrinding of the top roller, the load applied remains the same. The load is transmitted to the top arm via a fulcrum loading point.

The load on the bottom roller can be increased or decreased by simply varying the input air pressure. Over time, the hose inside the arm bar deteriorates and the load readings recorded with our Apex Top Arm Load Gauge have show erratic swings. In such a situation, the operators  increase the air pressure to compensate for the load reduction due to hose wear  or air leakage.

The loss of energy by way of increasing the air pressure is enormous. In a ring frame not all the loading arms will be problematic. The increase in air pressure will abnormally increase load in those top arms while marginally increasing in the defective arms.

A study showed that even a raise of 1 kg in the front roller of the top arm means a loss of Rs 9.5 lakhs to the mill for 25,000 spindles.

To overcome this problem, Apex has now introduced a mechanical spring powered drafting conversion kit for P3-1 top arms. A plate spring fastened on a distributor plate is mounted inside the arm bar. A screw protruding outside the arm bar on the bottom side allows for variation of loads.

The payback for this system starts from the day 1. The air line to the ring frame can be removed and the hose assembly inside the arm bar is removed. 

The yarn quality will also be steady since the mechanical load will be consistent unlike the pneumatic load.
